Nightshade Staff Guide

Welcome to the Nightshade Staff Team. This guide is your main resource for being a successful staff member, understanding expectations, supporting the community, and helping the city run smoothly.

Handle situations calmly. Stay professional. Be fair, but firm.

Onboarding

Onboarding Guide

New staff should be guided through expectations, Discord setup, ticket handling, logs, clips, and proper escalation.

Interview Questions
  • What inspired you to apply for staff?
  • How old are you?
  • What do you mainly do in city?
  • Are you in any approved whitelisted gangs?
  • Tell us a little about yourself.
  • What is your occupation or study IRL?
  • Can you balance staff with real-life obligations?
  • What is your availability and timezone?
  • What skills can you bring to the staff team?
  • How do you handle stress or high-pressure situations?
  • How do you help maintain a respectful community?
Final Onboarding Steps
  • Assign the proper Discord roles.
  • Invite them to logs Discord and assign correct roles.
  • Give them this guide and have them save it.
  • Walk them through a practice ticket.
  • Show them how to rename tickets, pull logs, ping Admin+, and move tickets.
  • Explain clip rules and exceptions.
Purpose

Mission & Staff Mindset

Nightshade staff are here to protect the community, support roleplay, and keep the city fair. Staff work should feel calm, consistent, and organized — never personal, messy, or emotional.

Community First

Every decision should help protect the experience of the city as a whole.

Fair Before Firm

Educate when possible, enforce when needed, and document when necessary.

Calm Authority

You do not need to argue to be respected. Clear, steady communication is enough.

Leadership

Chain of Command

Staff should follow chain of command to keep decisions consistent and prevent confusion.

Trial Support / Trial Mod

Handles basic questions, observes tickets, learns logs, and escalates when unsure.

Support / Moderator

Handles standard tickets, reports, and minor rule breaks within their permission level.

Admin

Handles escalated situations, bans, reimbursements, and staff guidance.

Senior Admin

Handles complex reports, appeals, disputes, and oversight-level issues.

Head Admin+

Final decision-making, staff discipline, major appeals, policy changes, and leadership.

When in doubt, escalate. Guessing can cause bigger problems than asking for help.
Availability

Staff Activity Requirements

  • Staff are expected to remain active and available when possible.
  • If you need a break, notify management instead of disappearing.
  • Tickets should not be ignored for long periods of time.
  • Staff should contribute to the team, not only hold the role for perks.
  • Repeated inactivity without notice may result in review or removal.
Core Rules

Staff Rules

Authority In-City

Never use staff title, rank, or influence for in-city advantages. In RP, you are treated like any other civilian.

Stay In Character

Remain fully in character unless there is a cheater, modder, or severe situation requiring immediate intervention.

Report Properly

Use the official ticket system and submit evidence. Do not call staff for help mid-RP.

Professional Conduct

Remain respectful even if a player is rude. No sarcasm, emotional arguing, insults, or power-tripping.

Admin Powers During RP

Do not spawn items, heal/revive yourself, teleport for RP advantage, give money/weapons, or remove consequences.

Teleporting & Visibility

Do not teleport into active RP without approval. Never use admin powers where civilians can see them.

Staff VC & Push-To-Talk

Staff must remain in staff VC while in-city and use push-to-talk or mute when roleplaying. Double Discording is prohibited.

Favoritism & Confidentiality

Zero tolerance for favoritism. Staff chats, tickets, punishments, investigations, and decisions are confidential.

Quick Reference

Staff Do's & Don'ts

Do

Stay calm, ask questions, review evidence, explain decisions, and escalate when needed.

Do

Document punishments, keep tickets organized, and communicate with the team.

Do Not

Argue, leak information, abuse permissions, favor friends, or make emotional decisions.

Accountability

Staff Discipline

Staff members are held to a higher standard because they represent the server. Misconduct may lead to warnings, strikes, demotion, removal, or bans depending on severity.

Minor Issues

Coaching, reminder, or verbal warning.

Repeated Issues

Strike, temporary permission restriction, or demotion.

Serious Issues

Immediate removal for leaking, power abuse, favoritism, or staff tool abuse.

Gang Angel Rules

Gang Angel Rules

  • Only 3 staff per gang.
  • Oversight cannot gang play; Support can.
  • No switching gangs without management approval.
  • Staff cannot be gang leaders, shot-callers, recruiters, war leaders, or alliance makers.
  • Staff duties come first.
  • Trial staff cannot join a gang until accepted.
  • Staff cannot moderate cases involving their gang/allies unless assigned.
  • No staff tools, spawning, or advantages for gangs.
  • Gang activity must be clipped and posted in the correct gang angel chat.
  • Failure to provide clips may result in a strike.
  • Staff receive double penalties and lower strike limits.
  • No retaliation for reports. Cheating results in cross-ban and staff removal.

Review Process

Appeals Process

  • Players may appeal eligible punishments through the proper appeal form or ticket.
  • Appeals should be reviewed by staff who were not directly involved when possible.
  • Staff should review clips, logs, prior history, and the original reason for punishment.
  • Appeal outcomes should be explained clearly and professionally.
  • Not all punishments are appealable, depending on the offense and guideline.
Clips & Proof

Evidence & Clip Requirements

Standard Clips

Clips should be at least 2 minutes long and include IDs when possible.

Exceptions

Cheating, slurs, exploits, and some F8 clips may be shorter if the evidence is clear.

Bad Evidence

Multiple tiny clips, missing context, no IDs, or cropped evidence may not be accepted.

Evidence should show context before, during, and after the rule break.
Compensation

Refunds & Compensation Rules

  • Refunds should only be given when there is proper proof.
  • Do not reimburse based on verbal claims alone unless management approves.
  • Use compensation stash tools when required by the ticket.
  • Document what was given, why it was given, and who approved it.
  • When unsure, escalate to Admin+ before issuing compensation.
Ticket Etiquette

Ticket Etiquette

Tickets are the main place players ask for help, report issues, or request support. Staff should keep every ticket respectful, organized, and professional.

A well-handled ticket can still leave a player feeling respected, even if the outcome is not what they wanted.
Ticket Rules

Ticketing Rules

  • Only one staff member handles a ticket at a time.
  • If the assigned staff does not respond within 30 minutes, another staff may take over unless requested/tagged.
  • Read the entire ticket before stepping in.
  • Remain neutral. If you have personal issues with a player, do not handle it.
  • Clips must be at least 2 minutes with IDs, except cheating, profane language, and some F8 scenarios.
  • Do not argue, use sarcasm, or make unnecessary comments.
  • If escalation is needed, tag the next in command.
  • If the ticket involves you, friends, or gang members, do not engage.
  • Ticket and admin chat information is confidential.
  • Respond to tickets promptly.

Ban Message

Help

Staff FAQ

What if I do not know the answer?

Be honest. Tell the player you are going to confirm with higher staff before answering.

What if a friend breaks a rule?

Treat them like any other player or pass the situation to another staff member if you feel biased.

What if a player is disrespectful?

Stay professional. Do not argue. Warn them if needed and escalate if they continue.

Can I use staff powers while roleplaying?

No. Staff powers are for staff duties only and should never create an RP advantage.
